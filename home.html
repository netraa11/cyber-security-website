Basic Security Tools

Here are some essential tools to help you stay secure in the digital world.

1. Kali linux

Kali Linux is a Debian-based distribution designed for digital forensics and penetration testing, featuring pre-installed security tools like Metasploit, Nmap, and Wireshark, popular among cybersecurity professionals.

2. Nmap

Nmap (Network Mapper) is an open-source tool used for network discovery and security auditing, capable of scanning ports, identifying services, detecting vulnerabilities, and mapping networks in cybersecurity.

3. VPN (Virtual Private Network)

A VPN encrypts your internet connection and hides your IP address, ensuring your online activities remain private and secure, especially on public Wi-Fi networks.

4. Password Manager

A password manager helps you create, store, and manage strong passwords. It ensures you use unique passwords for different accounts without having to remember them all.

5. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring a second form of verification, such as a code sent to your phone, in addition to your password.
